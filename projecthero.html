Empowering construction workers with digital tools

context
It’s a struggle for construction workers in India to get a job of their choice, to get paid what they deserve, to improve their work, life and financial condition and to carry their construction jobs with a sense of pride.
On the other side of the story, contractors and builders struggle to find good/productive workers for their projects, manage them and their payroll when they are deployed, and incentivize them monetarily to do a great job.
Projecthero (PH) is India’s first and largest construction workers platform and sets out to connect and solve above two sides of the problems.
my role
  • I joined PH to lead the product when they had just raised seed round of funding and was looking to develop Android app for workers and Web app for contractors.
  • Duration: March 2022 - July 2022
user research

Learning deeply about construction workers pain-points, behaviour and unexpressed needs

I conducted user interviews both on-site where heroes were staying and working as well as via video calls.
    I asked them questions to learn more about them and their problems:
  • How do you find a job?
  • What factors you consider when accepting a job?
  • How you travel to job city and site?
  • How do you maintain and remember daily number of hours and overtime?
  • How your salary is paid and in what cadence?
  • Which phone and apps do you use?
  • Do you have a bank account and insurance?
  • ...

Challenges with workers

  • Most constructions workers e.g. Mason, Plumbers, Shuttering etc. come from poor families and depend on their daily wage.
  • They are acquainted with smart phones via using common apps e.g. Whatsapp, YouTube, Facebook, Sharechat etc. but anything complex make them apprehensive.
  • They are anxious about money all the time remembering their daily hours and work either just mentally or noting them down on paper. Even if a Rs. 1 less is paid to them, it’s a matter of concern to them.
  • They understand Hindi or Hinglish language as most of them come from Eastern belt of UP.
  • They aspired for better accommodation and work conditions
  • They were not able to leverage their years of previous experience to get paid more

Design Solutions

Make plenty of jobs available to heroes. Heores depended on local workers market or labour contractor or relatives/friends to find a job. For the heroes, just being able to see so many jobs at their fingertip and apply to them was a first-ever and enriching experience.

We also captured job location preference from workers for better matchmaking.
